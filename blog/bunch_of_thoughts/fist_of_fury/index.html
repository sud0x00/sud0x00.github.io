My thoughts on Fist of Fury

        As a kid , I've always loved watching kung fu movies. I used to watch a lot of Jackie Chan , Donnie Yen  , Bruce Lee & Gordon Liu movies. And two of Lee's movies are my all time favorites ;  Enter the Dragon and Fist of fury.

I'm an outsider to all these incidents so as a kid all I noticed is that slant eyed people of two countries fight each other with a fighting form which involves soft touches and baseless screaming. On first glance Fist of Fury or Jing Wu Men might seem like any ordinary kung fu movie with an amazing title soundtrack by Joseph Koo but it has a lot to be explored.

                                                     

A young man seeks vengeance for the death of his teacher.


 Chen Jen's (Zhen) teacher is poisoned by the ruthless Japanese occupiers and Zhen is saddened by his shifu's demise. The Japanese come to the funeral and provoke the students of Jing Wu Men with a framed calligraphy which reads as “Sick man of asia” or 夫病亞東 fu bing ya dongan (in reality it's dongya bingfu). Of course any sane human would be angered if someone makes fun of a person who passed away recently. That's when he enters the dojo to beat up the Jappies.



 



 This scene caught my eyes while I rewatched it today. Keep in mind that Chinese here are compared to dogs and a poor dog catches Zhen's eye.

He realizes that his shifu was poisoned by a Japanese in this scene. He hangs the Chinese traitor and the Japanese cook after killing them both.



                                       

 

And guess what , you can see Zhen eating that dog later as revenge. How Chinese can this movie get wwwwww!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!

Zhen also kills the translator Wu. He does some recon as a telephone guy (he did an amazing job there) before he proceeds to the climax where he kills a dozen Japanese guys (the katana kill was amazing ) , an American who's supposed to be a Russian and a chad Japanese Suzuki (who's the main villain).

Only to realize that his fellow students of Jing Wu Men were killed. He surrenders , takes one last glance at the love of his life (yuan who looks amazing irl but trashy in the movie) and gets shot Walt Kowaski style while jumping.

 Now I'd like to talk some stuff about the cast and some final statements.

I watched Fist of Fury in Complete Chinese for the first time today and I really enjoyed it. I regret not watching it earlier. The earlier versions I saw were in English which really removed the vibe off of the movie. Even tho all I understood were ni hao , xie xie , wo ai ni , wo bu dong , shi , bu shi , hao , bu hao , jin tian & zhai jian ; the message the movie delivered is on a different level. So the above phrases and ching chong bing bong ai ya fu gu ah sai ye bu go ta zhi chi wo were all that's needed for me to get the vibes , I recommend you to watch the Chinese version.

Lee's Chinese suits him more than his English , his expressions and acting is to the point. You might find cringing at certain points but that's how you'd expect a person to act in those situations.

Nora Miao looks so gorgeous in real life but her character looks kiddish and irritating

Wei Ping who's a loyal dog of Japanese is born in Nanjiang. I laughed at the top of my lungs when I learnt that.

Chikahara Hashimoto Plays the role of a superhot villain but him speaking Japanese would've made the movie a bit more natural.

Robert Baker did a good job playing a russian . . . I'm sure that half the Chinese people thought that he was a Russian and Russians spoke English that well 😛
